## Next Actions

- P1: Extend the K=8-positive joint selector-propagator to K=16/32 with candidate generation trained directly against propagation loss and no-harm transfer. Deterministic structured candidates improve only K=16, no-harm margins suppress useful candidates when scoring is weak, score regression is negative, verification signatures help but do not solve safe deployment when merely concatenated, an explicit no-harm head collapses useful closure, and teacher-supervised safe generators remain insufficient.
- P2: Bounded delta parameterization is trajectory-positive, a branch-weighted target-local transition head gives a small 5-seed target-object improvement at an integrity cost, and simple channel-masked target-head constraints are negative; next step is state-validity-aware transition learning.
- P3: Add mechanism-aware propagation and long-horizon simulator rollouts; higher-budget N_bg=512 remains small-margin and N_bg=512 mechanism screens are mixed/negative.
- P4: Train mechanism-aware propagation, explicit shift detection, and selective adaptation on harder large-N held-out mechanisms.
- P5: Move beyond sparse-output gates: train calibration-aware mechanism uncertainty, branch calibration loss, and multi-step ECE/Brier/NLL.
- P6: Measure energy, allocator traffic, sparse-kernel behavior, Pareto frontiers, and trained matched-or-better speedups.
- P7: Run stronger closed-loop or multi-horizon corruption experiments and connect objectification components to downstream loss under larger branch/rollout effects.
